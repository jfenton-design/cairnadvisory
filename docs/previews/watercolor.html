08 · Watercolor system

The pigment, atomized.

The watercolor technique in our mark isn't just for the mark — it's a fingerprint we let surface as vista paintings at the head of long-form pieces, soft washes on hero surfaces, drop caps for editorial openers, and decorative pigment blobs. Companion, never headline. The mark itself is never atomized, faded, or blended — it always sits on linen at full fidelity.

Principles
01
Companion, never headline
Watercolor supports content. If you notice it before the headline, it's too loud.
02
Mark's palette only
Slate gray, warm amber, deep sienna. Never blue, green, pink, or saturated red.
03
Low opacity, by default
6–12% for washes and overlays. Never above 20% in body content.
04
Print & screen
Specs are CMYK-safe at the published opacity. Test on uncoated paper before shipping.
A standing rule · the mark is never atomized
Always full fidelity
The mark renders at 100% opacity, transparent background, every surface — hero corner, footer, favicon, signature. Same brightness, same contrast, every time.
Never atomized into fragments
The watercolor system below uses abstract pigment blobs and washes. The mark itself is never reduced in opacity, masked, cropped, or extracted into a stone fragment.

Vistas · the headline component for article heroes
Shoreline · the canonical example · hand-painted

A watercolor vista is the visual headline of a long-form note. It sits between the title block and the body, in the same palette as the mark, painting a scene that resonates with the piece — a shoreline for orientation, a mountain for the long view, a working block for operations. One vista per piece. Quiet. Atmospheric. Never decorative.

Authoring new vistas · commission recipe
Generation prompt

For new watercolor vistas — atmospheric scenes that head long-form pieces.

“A loose watercolor landscape vista on warm linen paper, hand-painted technique, wet-on-wet pigment bleed, soft graded washes. Palette restricted to: warm amber-sienna (#C4A87A, #8C5838), cool slate (#6B8A9A), deep umber (#52443A). Atmospheric perspective — distant elements very pale, foreground slightly more saturated. Subject: [shoreline / mountain ridges / river bend / row of brownstones / distant city skyline / warm restaurant interior]. Painterly edges, visible paper grain, no hard outlines. Generous negative space (top half largely empty paper). Editorial, restrained, contemplative — not picturesque or romantic. 1200×500 aspect.”

Always

  • Slate + amber + sienna palette only
  • Loose wet-brush edges, visible pigment pooling
  • Top ⅓ of frame mostly empty paper / sky
  • One subject, atmospheric perspective
  • 1200×500 (12:5), linen background visible

Never

  • Bright, saturated, or pastel color
  • Hard outlines, vector look, or digital cleanness
  • People, vehicles, signage, or text in the scene
  • Romantic or picturesque (sunsets, golden hour)
  • Dense, busy, or detailed compositions
